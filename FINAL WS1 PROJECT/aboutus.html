History of Tagas

Etymology of Brgy.Tagas

The word "tagas" is actually a Bicol term which means "hard." Accordingly,
people who are living in this place was known to be "hard-headed" which
caused others to call the place as "Tagas".

Geography and Demographics

Tagas is a barangay in the municipality of San Jose,
in the province of Camarines Sur. Its population as
determined by the 2020 Census was 2,022. This represented
4.60% of the total population of San Jose.
Barangay Tagas is classified as a rural place and is
composed of 7 puroks or zones. It is generally described
as plain and coastal. It is bounded to the north by Calalahan,
south by Casuna, Tigaon, Camarines Sur, east by Lagonoy Gulf,
and in the west by Salogon-Ponglon. Its major source of livelihood
is agriculture (farming and fishing). It has a total population
of 2,075, with 420 households, 440 families, and 1,009 registered voters.
